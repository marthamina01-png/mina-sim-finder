Quick Verdict — Which is Best for Your Family?

✅ Choose EE if…

You want the absolute best signal and 5G coverage in Croydon. You stream a lot and need reliability. You'd use perks like Apple Music or Netflix. You're also a BT broadband customer (bundle discount available).

✅ Choose O2 if…

You want great perks — O2 Priority gives early concert tickets and family discounts. Strong rural coverage if you travel outside cities. EU roaming (25GB) included free. Best customer service reputation of the three.

✅ Choose Vodafone if…

You travel abroad regularly — 50+ roam-free countries is the best of the three. Amazon Prime included free for 12 months. Save more if you bundle with Vodafone broadband. VeryMe app has regular high-street discounts.

⚠️ Watch Out For…

All three networks raise prices mid-contract. EE & O2 add £2.50/mo, Vodafone adds £1.80/mo each April. On 4 SIMs that's up to £10/mo extra you didn't budget for. Always ask: "What is the exact rise amount?" before signing.

✅ Before You Sign — 10-Point Checklist

1️⃣ Get the Price Rise in Writing

Ask: "What is my price in month 7 after the April rise?" Ofcom rules (Jan 2025) require networks to show this in £/pence at point of sale.

2️⃣ Check Croydon Coverage

Visit mastdata.com and enter your postcode. EE is typically strongest in South London for 5G.

3️⃣ Ask About EU Roaming

O2 includes 25GB free. EE charges £2.38/day. Vodafone charges £2.25/day (Zone B). Critical for family holidays to Europe.

4️⃣ Check Perks You'll Actually Use

EE Netflix/Apple Music only worth it if you'd pay for it anyway. O2 Priority is genuinely useful for concert families. Vodafone Amazon Prime = £9/mo value free.

5️⃣ Ask About Multi-Line

EE: £10 off SIM plans taken with a device. Vodafone: broadband bundle discounts. O2: check for family add-on deals at point of sale.

6️⃣ Understand Early Exit

All three charge remaining months if you cancel early. Some discount by a small percentage. Ask for the exact exit fee formula before signing.

7️⃣ Confirm eSIM

All three support eSIM. Ask at checkout — saves waiting for physical SIM. Requires iPhone XR+ or Samsung S20+.

8️⃣ Get PAC Code First

Text PAC to 65075 (free). Your current network replies in 60 seconds. Gives you 30 days to use it — and keeps negotiating options open.

9️⃣ After 12 Months

Contract rolls monthly. Set a phone reminder for month 11 to compare again — you'll often get a better deal by calling retention.

🔟 Consider Haggling

All three networks have retention teams. Call to say you're leaving — they often match competitor prices or add free perks to keep you.
